# WanderQuest — AI Handoff Guide

A walking AR adventure for the **RayNeo X3 Pro** smart glasses. This file
is written for a fresh AI model (or developer) taking over the project. Read
it fully before editing. The companion file `FABLE_X3_STARTER_GUIDE.md` (and
`docs/x3_dossier.md`) document the *hardware*; this file documents the
*game*.

---

## 1. What the game is

You wear the glasses and **walk in the real world**. A 16-bit fantasy world
overlays your view: treasure to find, mythological creatures to fight, a
story that unfolds the farther you roam. It is a single-player journey told
across "legs" (missions), built for all ages (the developer's daughter is
the target player).

**There is no GPS, no Google, no map service, no camera, no network needed
to play.** The world is driven entirely by the **pedometer** (step sensor) +
**head orientation** (rotation-vector sensor). This was a hard requirement
and earlier GPS/camera systems were deliberately deleted — do not reintroduce
them.

---

## 2. Design pillars (honor these)

- **It is NOT framed as an exercise app.** Distance is an *internal* pacing
  clock. Never show miles, step counts as goals, or fitness language. The
  story and rewards are the surface; walking is the hidden engine.
- **Deterministic, kid-proof controls.** Every mechanic must be simple and
  unambiguous. The team burned many iterations making head-gestures and
  navigation behave predictably; keep that bar.
- **The world always offers something.** Wherever the player stands, there
  is a task, a creature, a sign, an NPC, or a glimmer to walk toward.
- **Quality pixel art + chiptune.** Assets are procedurally generated by
  Python scripts (see §7) in three unlockable themes (fantasy/anime/western).

## 3. Controls (final, do not silently change)

Right temple pad is the main control; left pad is system volume + one game action.

| Gesture | Action |
|---|---|
| RIGHT **tap** | interact / pick up / strike / select — the universal verb |
| RIGHT **double-tap** | from the hunt → MAIN MENU; inside any screen/interaction → EXIT it |
| RIGHT **press & hold** | (reserved; menu is on double-tap from roaming) |
| RIGHT **swipe fwd/back** | switch the held weapon or wonder |
| RIGHT **swipe up/down** | radar zoom / navigate menu lists |
| RIGHT **triple-tap** | reset the compass trim |
| LEFT **tap** | use the held special item (tonic, elixir, smoke bomb…) |

**Combat motion gesture (gyro only, combat-only):** a sharp **head shake or
nod** = STRIKE in the battle screen. During a monster's lunge, **any head
movement** = DUCK. This is intentionally sensitive but ONLY listens during
combat, so looking around the world never triggers it. Tunable in
menu → Calibrate.

The right-temple "click" arrives as a hardware **KEY event**
(`KEYCODE_BUTTON_A`/`DPAD_CENTER`), not a touch — see `TrackpadGestureEngine`.
Light pad taps arrive as touch; both feed one unified tap resolver.

## 4. Core loops

1. **Navigate by destination lock.** Look at any distant object and **tap**
   to mark it. It pins to screen-center as a gold beacon and shows a live
   distance that ONLY ever counts down (`destDistM -= metersWalked` each
   GPS-style fix from the step world). Tap again to release. Walk any path;
   the distance falls. Within ~1 m it becomes grabbable.
   *(Implementation: `GameEngine.markDestination/advanceDestination`,
   `destRef/destDistM/destLabel`.)*

2. **Find loot, classified by importance** (color-coded):
   - green = **minor** (herbs/ingredients) ~every 10 m
   - blue = **medium** (gems/potions/scrolls/keys/rings/shrooms) ~every 100 m
   - orange = **high** (vaults, crowns, portals) ~every 300 m
   *(`GameEngine.rollDistanceSpawns`, `SpawnManager.spawnMediumItem/spawnHighItem`.)*

3. **Fight creatures.** ~1 enemy encounter per 50 m, denser near valuable
   ground (`dangerLevel()`). Ranged weapons (wands/rods/staves) are strong at
   range but **fumble within 4 m**; aggravated foes **sprint** at you;
   **ranged creatures** (slimes, imps, nagas, dragons…) kite and fling
   attacks from a distance. Aim within combat range (14 m) and tap/strike.

4. **Alchemy.** Herbs auto-craft into wonders the moment a recipe completes
   (`Alchemy.tryCraft`). Hold a wonder (swipe) and LEFT-tap to use it.

5. **Hearts & ghost.** Hearts = HP. At zero you become a **ghost** (grey
   world, monsters ignore you) until a Healing Pool, a Phoenix Feather, or
   time revives you.

6. **Journey legs / missions (THE headline goal).** Each leg has an internal
   average distance (leg 1 ≈ 1 mi, leg 2 ≈ 5 mi, then +1 mi each — never
   shown). As you walk, an **ordered story arc** of beats unfolds (Finn,
   villagers, signposts, town chatter). Each leg has **TWO boss WARDENS**
   (mythological, ~3× HP) that appear as the leg matures; **both must be
   slain to finish the leg**. Completing a leg banks coins, grants a Crown
   Shard, opens the next leg's story. *(`GameEngine.checkMission`,
   `ensureWardenTypes`, `onWardenDown`, `completeLeg`,
   `SpawnManager.spawnBossCritter`.)*

7. **Competition (offline).** Wander Duels: two players enter the same word
   code, compete 24 h, exchange checksummed result codes. Plus a retro
   **leaderboard web page** served from the glasses on the local network
   (`LeaderboardServer`, NanoHTTPD :19111, `assets/leaderboard.html`).

## 5. The bestiary (30 mythological creatures, 6 tiers)

`CritterType` (in `game/Models.kt`) is a 30-entry enum, each with
`tier (1–6), hp, xp, coins, ranged, rangedVerb`. Sprites are generated by
`tools/gen_creatures.py`. Difficulty scales with the leg:
`CritterType.bandFor(mission)` returns the tier range a leg may draw from,
and `ofTier(range, rng)` picks one. The two leg-wardens are drawn from the
top of the band. Tier 1 = Slime/Wisp/Pixie/Kappa/Imp … Tier 6 =
Cerberus/Manticore/Djinn/Wyrm/Hydra.

*(Note: `MonsterType` (ghoul/bat/wolf) is a SEPARATE small enum still used by
the thief/hunter "steal your satchel and flee" mechanic and the older
head-shake BATTLE screen. Field creatures use `CritterType`.)*

## 6. Code map (`app/src/main/java/com/tropicalstream/wanderquest/`)

```
MainActivity.kt        Activity wiring, 30fps frame loop, input dispatch, StepWorld
WanderQuestApp.kt      Application; optional reflective MercurySDK init
ui/BinocularSbsLayout  dual-draw 1280x480 → two 640x480 eyes (X3 binocular)
input/TrackpadGestureEngine  temple-pad gestures (click = KEY event!)
platform/
  StepWorld.kt         step dead-reckoning "GPS" (the ONLY world driver)
  HeadTracker.kt       rotation-vector yaw/pitch, head-shake/nod, angular speed
  StepTracker.kt       pedometer (detector→counter→accel fallback)
  FloorTracker.kt      barometer (dormant; indoor mode retired)
  Sfx.kt               SoundPool SFX + looping beds (ambient, heartbeat, drums)
  Geo.kt               haversine math + GeoFix (lat/lon are VIRTUAL/relative now)
game/
  GameEngine.kt        the brain: state machine, spawns, combat, legs, quests
  Battle.kt            head-shake/duck battle screen (thieves/hunters)
  SpawnManager.kt      world population: items, herbs, critters, wardens, signs
  Models.kt            CritterType(30), MonsterType, ItemType, HerbType, Place,
                       WorldItem, Critter, Signpost, Npc, Lever, Portal, Screen…
  Loot.kt              rarity tables, wander-power tiers, XP/level curve
  Gear.kt              weapons, armor, boots, guild shop
  Alchemy.kt           herb recipes → special items (auto-craft)
  Quests.kt            Lore: intro, leg arcs, beats, chatter, signs, villagers,
                       Finn quests, mileage-leg helpers
  DuelManager.kt       serverless duel codes (CRC-checked)
  StatsStore.kt        ALL persistent state (single JSON in SharedPreferences)
render/
  GameView.kt          640x480 black-canvas renderer (billboards, radar, HUD,
                       all menu/battle/dialog/guild/calibrate screens)
  SpriteBank.kt        loads themed sprites from assets/sprites/<theme>/
web/LeaderboardServer  NanoHTTPD :19111 "Tavern Notice Board"
```

**Rendering note:** pure black = transparent on the X3 waveguide. The whole
UI is one 640×480 logical canvas, duplicated to both eyes by
`BinocularSbsLayout`. Design at 640×480. Keep refresh ≤30fps (thermal).

**Persistence:** every gameplay field lives in `StatsStore` and is saved as
one JSON blob. When you add a stat, add it to BOTH `load()` and `save()` —
the verification script and reviewers check this symmetry.

## 7. Asset pipeline (Python — regenerate, never hand-edit PNGs)

```
tools/gen_sprites.py    items, gear, places, the 8 original enemies, UI icons
tools/gen_creatures.py  the 23 new mythological creatures (parametric)
tools/gen_sounds.py     ~57 chiptune WAVs → res/raw/
```
Each writes into `app/src/main/assets/sprites/<fantasy|anime|western>/` and
`app/src/main/res/raw/`. Run with Python 3 + Pillow + numpy. After adding a
creature/sound, also add its key to `SpriteBank.kt`'s load list and/or
`Sfx.kt`'s `rawByName` map, or it won't load.

## 8. Build & run (developer "Mars" runs these — see BUILD_AND_INSTALL.md)

The AI prepares edits and hands over a copy-paste block; the developer runs
`./gradlew` and `adb` in his own terminal. Single block:

```bash
cd ~/Projects/new-x3-app && ./gradlew :app:assembleDebug \
&& adb install -r app/build/outputs/apk/debug/app-debug.apk
```

Gradle 8.9 / AGP 8.7.3 / Kotlin 2.0.21 / JDK 17 / compileSdk 35, minSdk 29.
The vendor RayNeo/Mercury AARs are OPTIONAL (all vendor access is reflective);
the game runs without them. `local.properties` must point at the Android SDK.

## 9. Verification workflow (do this every change)

You (the AI) **cannot compile**. Before handing over a build:

1. Run the brace/paren/comment-balance + asset/sound cross-reference checker:
   `python3 <outputs>/verify.py` (it lives in the session scratch; recreate if
   missing — it strips Kotlin strings/comments and checks `{}()[]` balance per
   file, XML well-formedness, and that every `R.raw.*` / `sprites.get(...)` /
   `Sfx` map entry resolves to a real asset).
2. For non-trivial changes, spawn a sub-agent to do an **adversarial static
   review** (compile-breakers, unresolved refs, when-exhaustiveness,
   StatsStore load/save symmetry, logic bugs). This has caught real bugs
   every time; keep doing it.
3. Only then produce the build block.

## 10. Retired / do-not-revive systems

These were built then deliberately removed; don't bring them back without
the developer asking:
- **GPS / Google geolocation / IP geolocation / RayNeo IPC GPS** — replaced
  by `StepWorld` dead reckoning.
- **Camera / CameraX arm-motion detection** — replaced by gyro head gestures.
- **Indoor "Hearth Mode"** (auto-expanding hall, stairs/barometer) — retired;
  it's one outdoor world now. Dead code may remain but `stats.simMode` is
  forced false.
- **Beacon-lighting chapter objective** — replaced by the 2-warden leg gate.
- **Compass N/S/E/W tape** — directions are relative ("ahead", "to your
  left"); the radar minimap stays.
- **Mileage HUD bar** — the distance clock is now invisible.
